# Financial Literacy and Terminology

## Key Concepts

- Assets
- Liabilities
- Net worth

## Explanations

In addition to understanding financial concepts and terminology, it's also important to have a basic understanding of financial products and services. For example, knowing the difference between a savings account and a checking account can help individuals choose the right type of account for their needs. Similarly, understanding the concept of a budget and how to create one can help individuals manage their finances effectively.

## Examples

- Calculating net worth by subtracting liabilities from assets
- Understanding the difference between a credit score and a credit report
- Choosing the right type of bank account based on financial needs

## Summary Points

- Financial literacy is the ability to understand and manage personal finances effectively
- Assets, liabilities, and net worth are key financial concepts
- Understanding financial terminology and products is important for making informed decisions

## Self-Assessment Questions

Q: What is financial literacy?
A: The ability to understand and manage personal finances effectively

Q: What is the difference between assets and liabilities?
A: Assets are things that have value, while liabilities are debts or obligations that must be paid

Q: What is net worth?
A: The difference between assets and liabilities, representing an individual's overall financial health
